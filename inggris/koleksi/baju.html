The historical footprint of Makassar City as a Multi-ethnic City presents quite interesting facts about its diversity that took place since the beginning of the 16th century. parts of the world at that time. At the end of the 16th century to the beginning of the 17th century Makassar had become the commercial center of European countries and China, during the reign of Tunipalangga Ulaweng Raja Gowa to X (1546-1565). In the 20th century various ethnic groups settled in Makassar City including: Bugis, Makassar, Mandar, Toraja, Chinese, Arabic, Malay, Indian, Javanese, Banjar, Minahasa, Sangir, Ambon, Timor, Madura, Tanimba and Buton. The presence of various ethnic groups plays an important role in the economic sector in Makassar City and also plays a role in shaping the identity of Makassar City as a Multiethnic City.
