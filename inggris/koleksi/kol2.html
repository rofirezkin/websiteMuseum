Sultan Hasanuddin (XVI King of Gowa)

Sultan Hasanuddin is the 16th King of Gowa and the national hero of Indonesia who was born with the name Muhammad Bakir I Mallombasi Daeng Mattawang Karaeng Bonto Mangape as the name of a gift from the Islamic Qadi of the Gowa Sultanate namely Sheikh Sayyid Jalaludin bin Ahmad Bafaqih Al-Aidid. After ascending the throne he was named Sultan Hasanuddin, after he died he was given the title Tumenanga Ri Bakka Pangkana, because of his courage he was nicknamed De Haantjes Van Het Osten by the Dutch which means Rooster from the East.
Artifacts in the form of a Queen Wilhelmia statue and also a relief of the face of Queen Wilhelmia became an award from the Netherlands in Makassar City in 1906 where Makassar was considered to be a potential area in the Eastern Archipelago.
