Sultan Hasanuddin (XVI King of Gowa)

Makassar's ability to trade makes Makassar a highly developed trading port in Eastern Indonesia and attracts foreign traders to trade at the Makassar port. When the arrival of the Dutch Company (VOC) subdued Makassar and monopolized the spice market in Makassar & Maluku, Makassar became lethargic because it only became a haven for commercial ships from Maluku and Batavia. Due to the high demand for Chinese tea production in the European market, the Dutch Company established relations with China, with the arrival of Chinese traders who recognized Makassar and not Vlaardingen made the Makassar market grow again. Although the Dutch Company limited the number of junks that could sail to Makassar, but China was in dire need of marine production such as: Tripang, Shellfish, Pearl, Shark Fin, Hawksbill, and seaweed jelly made Chinese traders sell ceramics and porcelain to cheaper, even cheaper than their native China.
